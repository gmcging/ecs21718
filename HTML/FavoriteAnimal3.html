Some Fun Facts

Click
The anteater's scientific name Vermilingua means "worm tongue" in Latin. Giant Anteaters have been around for 25 million years. There is an estimated 5,000 left in the wild. while only 90 are in zoos. They eat mostly ants and termites as well as other insects. They vary in size too: some can be as small as a squirrel while others can grow to 7 feet long. The average lifespan of an anteater is 16 years. Their tongues can be as long as 2 feet. A certain type of anteater, the echidna, is one of the few mammals that lays eggs like the platypus.
